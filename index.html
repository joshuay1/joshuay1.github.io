Language

I am a Taiwanese-Australian with an Indigenous Taiwanese background. My family, of the Tsou People in Taiwan, has adopted a Han-Chinese surname and has been heavily Sinicised for multiple generations. Being unable to speak the Austronesian language that my ancestors once did, I always feel that there is a missing part of my personal identity. I understand how important language preservation is to the core cultural identiy of an individual.


On top of that, both my parents studied Anthopology. Since a young age, I have been participating in field trips and occasionally interviewing people from unique cultural backgrounds. I was exposed to the fascinating fields of Human languages and cultures since then. I am often intrigued by the unique culture of isolated tribes in remote areas.


Being born in Australia and raised in Hong Kong and Taiwan has also enriched my experience in terms of language learning. I am capable of communicating in English, Mandarin, Hokkien-Taiwanese and Cantonese fluently, and confortable with converstional Spanish. I initiated a Hokkien Taiwanese tutoring YouTube channel out of the concern of the preservation of the language (but failed to have the time to keep uploading).


My passion for language and linguistic is also the main reason that I switched from Software Engineering to Computer Science to explore more of the field of Natural Language Processing.
